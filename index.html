Centre for Theoretical Physics and Natural Philosophy Cosmology Group

The CTPNP Cosmology Group is focused on researching modified gravity, dark energy, and loop quantum cosmology. Our goal is to gain a better understanding of these topics and to develop new theories and models that can help explain the mysteries of the universe.

Research Areas

Dark Energy

Dark energy is a mysterious force that is thought to be responsible for the accelerating expansion of the universe. The CTPNP Cosmology Group is focused on studying dark energy in order to better understand its properties and behavior. We hope to develop new theories and models that can help explain this mysterious component and its effects on the universe. Through our research, the group aims to contribute to the broader field of cosmology and help deepen our understanding of the universe and its evolution.

Modified Gravity

Modified gravity is an alternative theory of gravity that seeks to explain the observed phenomena in the universe without invoking dark matter or dark energy. The CTPNP Cosmology Group is focused on researching modified gravity in order to better understand its properties and possible implications for our understanding of the universe. Through our research, the group hopes to contribute to the development of new theories and models that can help explain the mysteries of the cosmos.

Loop Quantum Cosmology

Loop quantum cosmology is a branch of theoretical physics that seeks to merge quantum mechanics and general relativity in order to better understand the behavior of the universe at the smallest scales. The CTPNP Cosmology Group is focused on researching loop quantum cosmology in order to develop new theories and models that can help explain the early universe and its evolution. Through their research, the group hopes to contribute to our understanding of the universe and its fundamental properties.
